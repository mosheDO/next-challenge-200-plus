                          CHILD'S PLAY

REQUIRES:

256 color video driver, Windows 3.1, VBRUN300.DLL.

INSTALLING:

1. Create a directory.
2. Copy the contents (a compressed ZIP file or a disk) into the 
   directory you created.
3. In FILE MANAGER, look into the directory you created. Look for 
   CHLDPLAY.EXE. Click this, drag it to the program manager and
   drop it into the Group of choice. This sets up the icon for you. 

INTRODUCTION:

Keep your kids busy for hours with the best paint/art program in the
shareware marketplace with Child's Play. It's so easy that your kids
will have this mastered in no time. More importantly, it is FUN!

Child's Play mixes art creation tools, screen erasers and special 
effects to bring out the young artist. This is the one program that 
kids never seem to tire of.

Child's Play takes full screen. This keeps kids from mousing over to
other places in Windows and potentially destroying something. Although
they can still Alt-Tab into another program, Child's Play is so mouse
intensive that it isn't likely that they will do this.

In addition, Child's Play is also an educational tool in that the mouse 
actions used to draw are the same ones used in programs used in adult
programs. Drawing rectangles, dragging stamps and other actions will help
prepare them for similar actions such as "drag and drop" later in life. 
Similarly, by supporting the right mouse button (although there is no
requirement to use it) your child becomes used to the idea that more than 
one mouse button can do things.

OPERATION: 

To operate, note that there are 2 3d pushbuttons on screen at the upper
left. The top one is the "menu" and the one underneath is UNDO. The 
menu button allows you to choose from a list of buttons on a 'flyout'
bar:

Draw Tools/Erasers/Effects/Stamps/Text/Load/Save/Print/Sound/Help/About/Exit

The HELP window will describe the operation of the first 3 sets of tools;
when you have selected DRAWING TOOLS or STAMPS the red arrow buttons on 
the left of the screen will cycle through the available choices. 

STAMPS will smear if the mouse button is being pressed and the mouse is 
moving. This is intentional; kids like the effect. Single stamps can be 
deposited once per mouse click.

TEXT will allow the child to type on screen.

SOUND toggles the sound effects off and on.

HELP brings up a window that describes the drawing tools, erasers, and 
special effects.

ABOUT tells you who we are and how to order. 

EXIT lets you quit the program.


COLORS:

Child's Play is a 256 color program and will use either mouse button. If you
look at the upper left of the screen, you'll see 2 boxes with color in them. 
They correspond to the colors assigned to the left and right mouse buttons. 
(Young children won't much care about the right mouse button, but the older 
kids will.) To select a color for the right mouse button, just choose a color
from the color palette at the screen top with the right button instead of the
left. You can access the rest of the color palette by clicking the boxes at 
the upper left described above; a window will pop up with the available color
spectrum.
